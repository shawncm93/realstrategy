Typography Rules

DO

  • Use Poppins for all headings
  • Use Inter for all body text
  • Maintain proper hierarchy (H1→H2→H3)
  • Use 16px minimum for body text
  • Limit line length to 65-75 characters

DON'T

  • Mix fonts inappropriately
  • Use Poppins for long body text
  • Use all caps for entire sentences
  • Stack more than 2 font weights
  • Stretch or condense letterforms

Voice & Tone

Our communication style is direct, confident, and human—never corporate or AI-sounding.

Brand Voice Attributes

AttributeDescriptionExample
DirectGet to the point, no fluff"We save clients $15K+ annually" not "We help optimize financial outcomes"
ConfidentExpertise without arrogance"Here's what works" not "We think maybe you could consider"
HumanConversational, not corporate"Your accountant doesn't get content creation" not "Traditional paradigms lack contemporary digital expertise"
SpecificConcrete over abstract"24-hour response guarantee" not "responsive service"

Writing Guidelines

✅ DO Write Like This:

  • Short sentences. One thought per sentence.
  • Concrete numbers. "$15K saved" beats "significant savings"
  • Active voice. "We'll handle your S-corp setup" not "Your S-corp will be handled"
  • Specifics over generalities. "1031 exchange for your Del Mar rental" not "tax-efficient real estate strategies"
  • Plain language. "Tax deadline" not "filing obligation terminus"

❌ DON'T Write Like This:

  • Em dashes everywhere—they make you sound like you're trying too hard—avoid them
  • "Delve," "nuanced," "comprehensive," "leverage," "seamlessly" (AI tells)
  • "It's worth noting that..." (just note it)
  • Excessive praise: "thrilled," "excited," "passionate" (show don't tell)
  • Neat three-part structures: "We believe X, Y, and Z" (forced symmetry)

Tone by Context

Website / Marketing

Direct, confident, benefit-focused. Lead with savings, speed, expertise.

"Unlike traditional accountants, we understand platform economies and content creation."

Client Communication

Professional but approachable. Explain without talking down.

"Your Q4 estimated payment is due Jan 15. Here's the breakdown."

Social Media

Casual, helpful, no jargon. Quick tips, real examples.

"Airbnb hosts: Your cleaning fees are deductible. Track them."

Forbidden Phrases

Never use these AI tells:

  • "Delve into" → Use "examine" or "look at"
  • "Leverage our expertise" → Use "use our expertise"
  • "Comprehensive suite of services" → Use "complete services"
  • "Seamlessly integrate" → Use "integrate" or "connect"
  • "Nuanced approach" → Be specific about what makes it different
  • "It's worth noting" → Just note it
